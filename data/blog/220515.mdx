In January 1957 a jury of four renowned architects unaniously selected Jorn Utzon's proposal as the winner of an international design competition. They lauded the Danish architect's proposal for a large-scale performace space as the "most original and creative solution" among the 230 proposals received form over 30 countries. The project had a very positive business case and was greenlighted. The estimated budget was $8 million and it would take four years to be constructed.

<br />
However, the intial deadline soon proved inadequate. A lack of definition and details
in Utzon's proposal made it unfeasible for engineers to start phase 1 in early 1959
as expected. The complexity of the design signifincantly increased contruction costs.
Project deadline were missed, the budget kept rising, and the work remaining on the
project seemed to be increasing over time rather than decreasing.
